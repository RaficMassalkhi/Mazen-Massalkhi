Expertise

Strategic Supply Chain Planning

It's essential to align with the organization's primary objectives and goals before establishing the Supply Chain Department's objectives, goals, strategies, and performance metrics.

Digital Transformation

Transitioning to advanced solutions like FnR, WMS, TMS, alongside harnessing AI, IoT, and Blockchain within supply chain technologies is critical. However, selecting the appropriate solution demands comprehensive due diligence, considering the organization's strategic direction and level of maturity.

Control Tower & Data Analytics

The challenge no longer resides in gathering data or executing complex analytical tasks but in identifying the crucial insights within data. Supply Chain KPIs must be synchronized with the organization’s KPIs and strategic objectives.

Risk Management & Supply Chain Resilience

Like any business sector, Supply Chain must proactively identify, evaluate, and address both internal and external risks, particularly for global operations. It's vital to distinguish between known and unknown risks, implementing appropriate countermeasures for each.

Supply Chain Sustainability

Supply Chain function plays a pivotal role in a company's sustainability efforts, with supply chain leaders often at the forefront of driving sustainability initiatives that support the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Logistics Network Design

Determining the ideal network configuration, capacity, and locations is crucial, requiring significant capital investments and strategic planning based on the organization's specific needs, limits, and ambitions.

Warehouse & Fulfillment Center Design

A successful design requires a thorough understanding of the organization’s strategic direction, existing challenges, and customer service commitments, utilizing the appropriate infrastructure.

Process Improvement & Operational Excellence

Pursuing the industry’s best practices in process improvement is fundamental to achieving operational excellence, ensuring an efficient and effective functioning of the supply chain.

Inventory Management & Control

Ensuring the accuracy of product databases and inventory levels, controlling inventory orders with methodologies such as Open-To-Buy (OTB) and ABC inventory analysis, and optimizing the product assortment through strategic listing and delisting are examples of tools that enhance inventory optimization and streamline its management.

Demand Forecasting & Replenishment

Striking the right balance between ensuring product availability and avoiding excess stock is crucial. This age-old challenge pits service levels against the cost of capital. Different industries and products require tailored forecasting methods.

Global Operations & Logistics Management

Mastering global logistics is a complex art that involves choosing the correct Incoterms, selecting carriers, determining the precise HS code, and adhering to export and import regulations to ensure swift market access and optimized landing costs.

Distribution Center Management

Optimizing the workflow from receiving to dispatch is crucial for effective operations. As DC Automation solutions evolve, it's recommended to evaluate the adoption of new technologies. DC associates should display a strong sense of responsibility, function with little oversight, and remain open to both process enhancements and technology updates.

E-Commerce & Omni-Channel Retailing

E-commerce has enabled retailers to expand their reach. Nevertheless, this has also placed them in direct competition with international retailers, intensifying the supply chain competition.

Q-Commerce

A form of e-commerce that focuses on delivering goods to consumers with unprecedented speed, calls for strategically located inventory locations (dark stores) to minimize delivery times and maintain efficiency. This service requires convenience, speed, and flexibility.

Last Mile at Customer Service

The last mile delivery is crucial as it represents the final step in the customer's journey and must be executed flawlessly. The key to success in last-mile delivery lies in the delivery team's attitude and their alignment with the company's customer service values.

Fulfillment Center Management

While the core activities of a fulfillment center: picking, packing, and shipping, may seem straightforward, it's vital to maintain their simplicity and ensure that every step of the process is optimized. This applies to the applications used in these operations for maximum efficiency.
